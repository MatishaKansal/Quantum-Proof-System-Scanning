Software Requirement  Specification (SRS) 
 
 
❖ Introduction  
 
 
      1.1 Purpose 
The purpose of this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document is to 
identify and document the user requirements for Quantum -Proof Systems 
Scanner. This document  provides a comprehensive description of the system 
requirements, expected functionality, and operational behavior of the proposed 
solution. It acts as a formal reference for stakeholders including system 
developers, cybersecurity teams, administrators, and management involved in 
the planning, design, development, and de ployment of the system. 
The SRS establishes a clear understanding of how the system will operate, the 
services it will provide, and the constraints under which it will function. It also 
serves as a baseline for system design, implementation, testing, and future 
enhancements. 
This document is prepared  with the following objectives: 
• To describe the behavior and operational functionality of the system. 
• To define the functional and non-functional requirements of the system. 
• To provide process flow charts and workflow representations for 
understanding system operations. 
• To provide a structured reference for system design, development, testing, 
and deployment. 
• To ensure that the system meets the required cybersecurity, compliance, 
and operational standards for identifying and managing cryptographic 
configurations in public-facing applications. 
 
 
   1.2 Scope 
The proposed system Quantum -Proof Systems Scanner is designed to enhance 
the cybersecurity posture of public-facing banking applications by identifying 
cryptographic implementations and evaluating their readiness against emerging 
quantum computing threats. The system will function as an automated scanning 
and monitoring platform capable  of discovering cryptographic assets, analyzing 
security configurations, and providing actionable insights for improving 
cryptographic resilience. 
The scope of the system includes the following functionalities: 
 
• Discover cryptographic inventory associated with public-facing 
applications, including TLS certificates, VPN endpoints, and APIs 
exposed to the internet. 
• Identify and analyze cryptographic controls implemented within the 
system, such as cipher suites, key exchange mechanisms, encryption algorithms, 
and TLS protocol versions. 
• Validate whether the deployed cryptographic algorithms and 
configurations comply with Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) standards 
recommended by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). 
• Generate detailed Cryptographic Bill of Materials (CBOM) inventory for 
identified assets in accordance with guidelines provided by Indian Computer 
Emergency Response Team (CERT-In). 
• Provide actionable recommendations and remediation guidance for assets 
that are not compliant with PQC standards or contain weak cryptographic 
configurations. 
• Issue automated digital labels and security status indicators such as 
Quantum-Safe, PQC Ready, or Fully Quantum Safe based on the cryptographic 
posture of each scanned asset. 
• Provide an enterprise-wide centralized management console through a 
secure graphical user interface (GUI) that allows administrators and 
cybersecurity teams to monitor, manage, and analyze the cryptographic status of 
all scanned public-facing applications. 
• Display risk ratings and security scores (High, Medium, Low) for each 
application based on its cryptographic configuration and compliance status. The 
dashboard dynamically reflects changes in security posture whenever 
cryptographic configurations are updated or vulnerabilities are identified. 
• Enable reporting and visualization of scanning results, cryptographic 
inventory, compliance status, and recommendations through the centralized 
dashboard interface.

❖ Overall Description 
 
2.1 Product Perspective  
The proposed system Quantum-Proof Systems Scanner is designed as an 
advanced cybersecurity solution aimed at identifying, analyzing, and managing 
cryptographic implementations used in public-facing banking applications. It 
functions as a centralized scanning and monitoring platform capable of detecting 
cryptographic configurations, evaluating their security posture, and assessing 
their readiness against emerging threats posed by quantum computing.  
The system operates as a security assessment and compliance  tool that 
integrates with  existing banking infrastructure without disrupting live 
services. It continuously analyzes internet-exposed assets, including: 
• Web servers 
• APIs 
• VPN gateways 
• Other externally accessible services 
 
Through  this analysis, the platform detects and evaluates: 
 
• Cryptographic protocols 
• SSL/TLS certificate configurations 
• Encryption mechanisms used in secure communication 
 
The proposed  solution follows a modular architecture  consisting of several 
specialized components: 
• Asset Discovery Module – identifies and catalogs internet-facing assets. 
• Cryptographic Scanner – detects cryptographic protocols, cipher 
suites, and certificates in use. 
 
• Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) Validator – evaluates the 
readiness of current cryptographic systems against quantum 
computing threats. 
• Cryptographic Bill of Materials (CBOM) Generator – generates a 
structured inventory of cryptographic components used across applications. 
• Enterprise Monitoring Dashboard – provides a centralized 
interface for monitoring risks, viewing analytics, and generating 
compliance reports. 
 
The system aligns with Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) recommendations 
from the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) and 
generates cryptographic  inventories following CBOM guidelines issued by the 
Indian  Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT -In). 
 
By enabling automated  detection,  classification, and reporting of 
cryptographic assets , the system helps organizations proactively prepare 
for the transition to quantum -safe cryptographic systems . The product 
ultimately functions as an enterprise-level monitoring platform that 
provides centralized visibility, risk evaluation, and compliance  
reporting for public-facing applications within the banking ecosystem. 
 
2.2 Product Functions 
 
The proposed system will perform a set of advanced functions designed to 
detect cryptographic implementations and evaluate their security posture 
in relation to quantum -safe standards. The major functions of the system 
include the following: 
• Asset Discovery: Identify and catalogue public-facing applications 
such as web servers, APIs, VPN gateways, and other internet -
exposed services.  
• Cryptographic Inventory Discovery : Extract and document 
cryptographic elements including TLS certificates, encryption 
algorithms, cipher suites, key exchange mechanisms, and TLS protocol 
versions used by the identified assets. 
• Cryptographic Analysis: Analyze the detected cryptographic 
configurations to identify weak, deprecated, or non -compliant 
encryption mechanisms. 
 
• Post-Quantum Cryptography Validation: Evaluate cryptographic 
algorithms against PQC standards defined by the National Institute of 
Standards and Technology (NIST) to determine their resistance against 
potential quantum computing attacks.
 
• CBOM Generation : Automatically generate a Cryptographic Bill of 
Materials (CBOM) containing a detailed inventory of cryptographic 
assets and  configurations as recommended by Indian Compute     Emergency 
Response Team (CERT -In). 
• Risk Assessment and Scoring: Assign risk ratings such as High, 
Medium, or Low to each application based on the strength of its 
cryptographic con figuration and its compliance with PQC standards. 
• Recommendation  Engine: Provide remediation guidance and 
actionable recommendations for systems that are not compliant 
with quantum-safe cryptographic standards.  
• Quantum-Safe Certification: Automatically issue digital security 
labels such as Quantum-Safe, PQC Ready, or Fully Quantum Safe for 
applications that meet predefined cryptographic requirements. 
• Enterprise Monitoring Dashboard: Provide a centralized graphical 
console that displays the real -time security posture, scanning results, 
cryptographic inventory, and compliance status of all monitored 
systems. 
• Reporting and Exporting : Generate detailed security reports and 
export them in machine-readable formats such as JSON, XML, and CSV 
for integration with other security monitoring systems.

2.4 Operating Environment  
 
The operating environment for the Quantum-Proof Systems Scanner consists of 
the hardware and software infrastructure required for the deployment, 
execution, and operation  of the system. The system will be deployed within a 
secure banking network environment and will support centralized monitoring 
and scanning of public -facing applications. 
The operating environment for the system is listed below: 
• Server System: High-availability enterprise server or virtual machine 
infrastructure capable of supporting large -scale network scanning and 
cryptographic analysis. The server must support secure communication 
and high processing capability to handle multiple scanning tasks 
simultaneously. 
• Operating System : Linux-based operating systems such as Ubuntu 
Server, Red Hat  Enterprise Linux (RHEL), or CentOS are recommended 
due to their stability, security features, and compatibility with 
cybersecurity tools. 
• Database : A relational database management system such as 
PostgreSQL or MySQL  will be used to store cryptographic inventory 
data, scan results, CBOM records, user activity logs, and security 
reports. 
• Platform: The system will operate as a web -based application platform 
accessible through a secure browser interface. It will support centralized 
access for administrators and cybersecurity teams through an 
enterprise dashboard.  
 
• Technology: The system will be developed using modern web 
technologies and cybersecurity tools such as Python / Java for backend 
processing, RESTful services for communication, and HTML5, CSS, and 
JavaScript for the frontend interface.  Cryptographic analysis tools and 
security libraries will be integrated to support TLS scanning and 
algorithm validation. 
• API: The system will utilize secure REST APIs for communication 
between modules, integration with external security tools, and 
interaction with the enterprise  dashboard.  APIs will also enable 
automated data exchange with other security monitoring systems if 
required. 
• This operating environment ensures that the proposed  system can 
operate ef ficiently, securely, and reliably within the banking 
infrastructure while supporting advanced cryptographic scanning 
and monitoring capabilities.

❖ Specific Requirements  
 
3.1 Functional Requirements  
 
 
The functional requirements describe the core operations and services that the 
system Quantum-Proof Systems Scanner must perform. These requirements 
define how the system will detect cryptographic con figurations, analyze security 
posture, and provide actionable insights regarding quantum -safe readiness of 
public-facing banking applications. 
The major functional requirements of the system are as follows: 
 
▪ Asset Discovery: The system shall identify and catalogue public -facing 
applications including web servers, APIs, and VPN endpoints that are 
exposed to the internet. 
 
▪ Cryptographic Inventory Discovery : The system shall automatically 
detect cryptographic elements such as TLS certificates, encryption 
algorithms, cipher suites, key exchange mechanisms, and TLS protocol 
versions used by the identi fied assets. 
 
▪ Cryptographic Configuration Analysis: The system shall analyze 
detected cryptographic configurations to identify weak, deprecated, or 
insecure encryption mechanisms. 
 
▪ Post-Quantum Cryptography Validation : The system shall evaluate the 
detected cryptographic algorithms against Post -Quantum Cryptography 
standards recommended by the National Institute of Standards and 
Technology (NIST) to determine whether the asset is quantum -safe. 
 
▪ CBOM Generation: The system shall generate a Cryptographic Bill of 
Materials (CBOM) inventory that lists all cryptographic components and 
configurations associated with public-facing applications in accordance 
with guidelines provided by Indian Computer Emergency Response Team 
(CERT-In). 
 
▪ Risk Assessment and Rating: The system shall assign risk levels such as 
High, Medium, or Low to each application based on the strength of its 
cryptographic con figuration and compliance with PQC standards. 
 
▪ Recommendation Engine : The system shall provide actionable 
recommendations and remediation guidance for systems that are not 
compliant with quantum-safe cryptographic standards.  
 
▪ Dashboard Monitoring: The system should provide a centralized 
dashboard that displays cryptographic inventory, security status, risk 
ratings, and compliance information for all scanned assets.  
 
▪ Report Generation : The system shall generate detailed reports containing 
cryptographic inventory, vulnerability analysis, compliance status, and 
security recommendations. 
 
▪ Security Label  Generation: The system shall automatically issue digital 
labels such as Quantum-Safe, PQC Ready, or Fully Quantum Safe for 
applications that meet predefined security criteria.

Security is a critical component of the proposed system <Project Name>, as it 
operates within a sensitive banking environment and interacts with public -facing 
applications. The system must ensure protection of data, secure communication, 
controlled access, and compliance with organizational and regulatory 
cybersecurity standards.

development, deployment, and operation of the system.

The proposed system must be compatible with the existing IT infrastructure of the 
organization. Integration with current systems, networks, and security tools 
should be performed without disrupting ongoing banking operations. The 
implementation should ensu re that existing systems remain unaffected and 
continue functioning normally during and after the deployment of the proposed

The system should maintain comprehensive audit trails for all critical operations

• Date and time of activity 
• Type of operation performed  
• System response  and status

on user roles and responsibilities.  
Security principles such as segregation of duties and need-to-know access must be 
enforced. For example:

view reports.

dashboards.
